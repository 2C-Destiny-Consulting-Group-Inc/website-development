Doris “DeeDee” Banks

Doris "DeeDee" Banks is a seasoned child welfare professional with over 20 years of experience advancing quality improvement, workforce development, and family-centered care. Her career began with the Florida Department of Children and Families, where she held roles such as Family Care Manager, Child Protective Investigator, and Adoptions Program Specialist. She later joined the lead Community-Based Care agency as Staffing Master and Director of Quality and Utilization Management, leading efforts to strengthen practice and system accountability.

DeeDee has also served as a staff development leader, family preservation supervisor, and community facilitator for parenting and decision-making programs. She holds dual bachelor’s degrees in Sociology and Music from the University of Florida and a Master’s in Nonprofit Management from the University of Central Florida. She is certified as a Child Welfare Trainer, Case Manager Specialist, and holds credentials in TBRI® and CPI. Her work reflects a strong commitment to building resilient families and empowering child welfare professionals.
